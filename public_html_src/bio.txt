learning interpretability and security for protecting data in the face of LLM apps tuned to
overshare. Previously he worked at Truera where he developed TruLens, an open-source toolkit for
evaluating LLM applications. Prior to Truera, he was a researcher at Carnegie Mellon University and
University of Maryland where his research included interpretability and accountability for privacy
and fairness in machine learning pipelines; and probabilistic programming and formal methods.
